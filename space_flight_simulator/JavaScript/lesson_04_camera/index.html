Lesson 4 — Camera Orientation: Pitch and Yaw

Euler Angles
We describe the camera's rotation with two angles:
  • Yaw — rotation around the world Y axis (looking left/right)
  • Pitch — rotation around the camera's local X axis (looking up/down)
We skip roll (tilting sideways) — that keeps the math simple and matches how flight simulators work. Together, yaw and pitch define every direction you can look without rolling.
Camera Basis Vectors
From yaw and pitch we derive three unit vectors that define the camera's local frame:
  • forward — the direction the camera is pointing
  • right — 90° to the right of forward, in the horizontal plane
  • up — perpendicular to both (points toward the sky)
These three vectors together are what a view matrix encodes in OpenGL. Here we compute them directly from trigonometry, so you can see the math.
World → Camera Transform
To draw a world point, we first express it in the camera's local frame: camX = dot(right, point − cameraPos)
camY = dot(up, point − cameraPos)
camZ = dot(forward, point − cameraPos)
Taking the dot product with each axis projects the vector onto that axis — the same operation as multiplying by the view matrix. After this, camZ is depth and we use the same projection formula as Lesson 3.
Controls
↑ pitch up   ↓ pitch down   ← yaw left   → yaw right
Camera is still fixed at the origin — movement comes in Lesson 5.
