Lesson 5 — Thrust and Physics

Newton's Laws in Zero Gravity
1st law: an object in motion stays in motion — in space there's no air friction, so once you're moving, you keep moving forever unless you apply a reverse thrust.
2nd law: F = m·a — force equals mass times acceleration. Since we treat the ship's mass as 1, force and acceleration are the same thing. Pressing a thrust key adds to acceleration, not directly to velocity.
Thrust Applies Acceleration, Not Velocity
Here's the key difference from Lesson 2:
acceleration += thrustDirection × THRUST_FORCE   (while key held)
velocity += acceleration × dt   (every frame — integrating acceleration)
position += velocity × dt   (every frame — integrating velocity)
This two-step integration (Euler integration) is the foundation of all game physics engines. Thrust direction comes from the camera's local forward and right vectors, so WASD pushes you in whatever direction you're facing.
Controls
W / S thrust forward / backward   A / D thrust left / right
↑ / ↓ pitch   ← / → yaw
